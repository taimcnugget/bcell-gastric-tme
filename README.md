# B Cell Dynamics in Gastric Cancer Tumor Microenvironment (TME)

## Motivation/Biological Question
* According to the American Cancer Association estimates about 32,000 new cases of stomach cancer in 2026 with a predicted 11,000 becoming fatal. [1](https://www.cancer.org/cancer/types/stomach-cancer/key-statistics.html)
* Like many immune cell types, B cells play a dual role in cancer progression. B cells facilitate proliferation, differentiation, and migration of tumor cells in addition to playing a role in anti-tumor immunity by producing antibodies that target cancer cells, thereby activating other immune cells to attack cancerous cells. 
    * Most importantly, tumor microenvironment (TME) are highly hetergeneous from patient to patient and from organ to organ, which makes developing immunotherapeutics difficut - since patients will have varying responses based on their TME. 
* How do B cell subsets (naive, transitional, memory, and plasma cells) change across healthy, primary, and metastatic gastric cancer 

## Dataset
GSE163558 - 10 scRNA-seq samples from gastric cancer patients spanning primary tumor, healthy tissue, and metastatic sites (liver, lymph node, ovary, peritoneum). The original paper focused on overall TME composition; this analysis extends their work with a focused investigation of the B cell compartment. The PDF of the original paper can be found in the data folder of this repo.
